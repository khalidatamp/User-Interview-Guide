User Interview Questions: A guide

Setting the Interview

To set an interview properly, we need to address these two questions

  • How do we make an interview feel comfortable?
  • What does the user know about the process and purpose?

Direct v indirect questioning

  • Direct questioning: interviewers explain precisely what they hope to discover
  • Indirect questioning: interviewer might not be completely open about what they're trying to find out - only revealing their aims during the debrief
  • We should be careful with this approach and consider the ethical boundaries when planning our questions

The Interview

Research Questions

  • What we're trying to find out
  • Research questions can be extremely direct, rigid and use specific language

Interview Questions

  • Research questions re-written for the user
  • Less direct and less rigid

Example

Research question Interview Question
What value might users find in the news feed? Is there anything you like about the news feed?

Example

Research question Interview Question
How can we use reminders to help new users stick to their medication schedules? Have you put all of your medication into the app?
  Do you use reminders for these medications?
  How useful do you find these?

Preparation

Interview preparation should involve creating 2 sets of questions

  • Set of Research questions
  • Set of Interview questions

Note: It's not a 1:1 ratio of questions

Preparation

Interview preparation should also involve consideration of order of questioning

What -> How -> Why

What makes a good question?

Short answer

  • A good question depends on what you're trying to acheive

Long answer

  • We need to understand Thematic v dynamic contributions

The thematic dimension

The thematic dimension of a question is how much it helps our understanding of the research questions

The dynamic dimension

The dynamic dimension of a question deals with the interpersonal aspect of questioning

Example

Assume our research question is: How can we improve user navigation experience to encourage exploration of lesser used features?

Dynamic Interview Question Example

A more dynamic questions is short and without any jargon

  • Good example
    • "What was good about navigating the app?"
  • Bad Example
    • "If you consider a page flow on the screen, what was your most positive experience of …"

More Thematic Interview Question Example

A stronger thematic question doesn't stray from the research question

  • Good Example
    • "Do you find it difficult to reach certain features?"
  • Bad Example
    • "How do you feel about the menu bar being on the bottom now?"

'What' questions

What questions are richer in their thematic contributions

  • Descriptive, open-ended questions

'What' examples

Assume our research question is: How can we improve user navigation experience to encourage exploration of lesser used features?

Examples

  • What parts of the app do you rarely use?
  • What happened when you tried to press the back button?

'How' questions

How questions are richer in their dynamic contributions

  • Encourage conversation about feelings and subjective experience

'How' examples

  • How well do you feel you can find what you're looking for in the app?
  • How questions don't have to begin with the word 'how'
    • E.g. "How did you feel" is roughly the same as "What did you experience"

'Why' questions

Why questions help us understand the user's reasoning

  • Asked first in research questions
  • Asked last in interview questions
  • Encourage speculation

'Why' examples

Assume our research question is: How can we improve user navigation experience to encourage exploration of lesser used features?

Examples

  • Why did you go back to the dashboard to open courses?
  • Why did you not just use the courses button in the bottom navigation?

Debriefing

Steps to debriefing:

  1. Ask if the user has anything further to share
  2. Ask how the user felt about the interview process
  3. Share information about the research questions/aims
  4. Share the things interviewer(s) learned during the interview (look out for corrections)
